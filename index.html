Hi! I'm Mike Wu, a machine learning enthusiast, researcher, and entrepreneur.

My interests are primarily related to a mixture between generative models and neural networks. I studied at Yale where I strived to encourage computer science on campus. I cofounded YHack, Yale's annual 2000 person international hackathon; supported CodeBoola, a coding bootcamp for New Haven high school students; and served as a teaching assistant for both the management and computer science schools.

I've jumped around a lot from field to field. Along the way, I've worked as a data scientist at Ionis Pharma; researched at Yale, Harvard, and Oxford; and cofounded Invrea, a probabilistic programming language built directly in Microsoft Excel. Starting in December, I will be working as a research engineer at Facebook Research.

One of my favorite pastimes is blogging. I write about a spectrum of topics from machine learning to art to psychology. I'd love to get any feedback possible.
