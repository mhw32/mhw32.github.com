Hi! I'm Mike Wu, a machine learning enthusiast, researcher, entrepreneur, and artist.

I'm a soon-to-be Ph.D. student at MIT starting Fall 2017. My interests are primarily related to Bayesian nonparametrics, neural networks, and time-series predictions. Before that, I was a student at Yale where I strived to encourage computer science on campus. I cofounded YHack, Yale's annual 1500 person international hackathon; supported CodeBlue, a coding bootcamp for New Haven high school students; and served as a teaching assistant for classes in both the management and computer science schools.

I've jumped around a lot from field to field. Along the way, I've worked as a data scientist at Ionis Pharma; researched at Yale, Harvard, and Oxford; and cofounded Invrea, a probabilistic programming language built directly in Microsoft Excel.

One of my favorite pastimes is blogging. I write about a spectrum of topics from machine learning to art to psychology. I'd love to get any feedback possible.
