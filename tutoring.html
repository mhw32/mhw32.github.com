Private Computer Science Tutoring

Hi I'm Mike! I'm a recent graduate from Yale University with B.S. in Computer Science. My tutoring services are for students of all skill levels: whether it's your first time coding, or you are jumping into machine learning, I'm happy to help guide you along the way.

Available Topics

Depending on the student's needs, I can either provide directed sessions based on my own curriculum, or I can offer more freeform tutoring that centers around the student's current interests. Below are some suggested topics that students can choose to start off from.

Intro to Programming

This is intended for beginners who have little or no experience in programming. Students will learn Python and basic computer science concepts.

Data Structures and Algorithms

This is intended for students familiar with basic coding. In our sessions, we will focus more on the theory/math behind computer science, exploring complexity and design behind widely used data structures and algorithms. Students will apply the concepts learned in coding exercises.

Web Development

This is intended for students familiar with basic coding. Students will learn the principles behind web applications and use Flask/Node to build their own app.

Guided Project

This is for students who are interested in building an idea of their own but may need help along the way. Like office hours, I can help the student break down their idea into steps, and assist with any coding/conceptual problems along the way.

Homework Help / Test Practice

I'm also available to help students who are looking for help with problem sets or homework or preparation for an important exam.

About Me (CV)

I've been studying computer science since early high school, so I've had a lot of time to explore the field. I started learning computer science by building a blind spot detector to help me drive. I was a Siemens semifinalist and Intel finalist.

I graduated with honors in computer science from Yale University in 2016 and have also taken several courses at the University of Oxford. I've worked in 5 artificial intelligence research labs at UCSD, Yale, Harvard, MIT, and Oxford, and have published 5 machine learning papers (still going!) and 2 patents.

So far, I've interned at two companies, started my own machine learning venture, and created YHack, one of the largest collegiate hackathons in the world. In my free time, I work on side projects and blog.
