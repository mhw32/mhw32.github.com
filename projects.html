What I hack on in my free time.

These fun mini-projects are my way of taking a step back from the world of research and delve into applications. These projects are a hobby that lets me play with the latest tech in web apps, chrome extensions, and hardware.

Software / Web Apps

Rambrandt: A Generative Model over Modern Art

Modern art generation through pixel based Bayesian N-gram switching state model. Results are passable as simple modern pieces but more complex models can be used to create more intricate artworks! A blog post of the work can be found on the about page.

Aflume: A Music Subscription Service for Fans

A former venture with the purpose of bringing music artists closer to their fans. We worked with a handfull of artists to try the Aflume subscription model, where fans support their favorite artists financially, and in return artists curate a "personal" musical experience (including special EPs, meet-and-greets, unreleased images, etc.)

Airbox: Rent Out your Extra Dropbox Space

A fun hack from HackMIT 2014. Using Airbox as a space seller, you could allot space for random users to place files. Using Airbox as a space buyer, your files are encrypted (AES) and segmented into several sellers' spaces. Ignore the obvious legal issues. Awarded Top 8 projects at HackMIT 2014, Dropbox API Award.

Penpal: An Intelligent Assistive Writing Tool

Completed a prototype of a combined hardware and software solution intended to aid individuals with essential tremor when writing. Much of the work is on signal processing, regression learning, and structural design. Iterated through multiple stages of prototypes!
