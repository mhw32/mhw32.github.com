Software / Web Apps

Sensei

Sensei is an EdTech hack that tracks the emotions of students in an online classroom as they watch a lecture or participate in discussion. Sensei tries to address the problem of engagement by more directly measuring it through analyzing a webcam feed of the student. As the student watches the lecture, Sensei extracts his/her emotion i.e. angry, happy, neutral, etc as a proxy for whether the student is frustrated, excited, disengaged, etc. This information for each student is relayed back to the course content creator and may be useful for future content creation, personalized lessons, or tracking progress.

Ada

PageRank orders pages by number of forward and backward URL references. It would be more optimal to organize the knowledge in the web semantically based on the content within text. Ada is a WIP AI that's a frontend for the web. It runs on an NLP library (similar to coreNLP) built solely on recurrent neural networks.

Sentiment Buddy

A tool for sentiment analysis using a neural network (Convolution + LSTM) trained on IMDB and MDSD data to obtain 0.89 classification rate. Embeddings (features) from sentences as strings into the vector space are learned automatically by the network.

Airbox: Rent Out your Extra Dropbox Space

A fun hack from HackMIT 2014. Using Airbox as a space seller, you could allot space for random users to place files. Using Airbox as a space buyer, your files are encrypted (AES) and segmented into several sellers' spaces. Ignore the obvious legal issues. Awarded Top 8 projects at HackMIT 2014, Dropbox API Award.
